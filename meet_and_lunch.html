MEET AND LUNCH
Problem Space Networking plays an every important role in career. Many people work in a company-concentrated areas but lack of a opportunity to get to know others who work in same industry. Traditionally, people build networks through alumni network, Meetup, LinkedIn or friends. It is hard to build personal connections through these mediums. People want an easier way to meet other professionals and build connections.

User Research
I interviewed five target users in person. I asked interviewees about their work and life to gain insights about their lifestyles and their ways of social networking. I identified LinkedIn, Meetup and friends are the main ways to meet new people.
  • Offer background information
  • Can connect to targeted people
  • Low response rate
  • Too formal, hard to make friends
  • Easy access to events
  • Can join different groups
  • Don't know people's backgrounds
  • Too many people per event, hard to bond
  • Little control of time and location
  • Quality hangouts
  • Likely to bond
  • Limited choices
Ideation
During the interview, I learned some interviewees hang out with co-workers during lunch time while others have lunch alone. People often buy lunch from places near work, but the choices are very limited and food is usually unhealthy and insipid. People(Chef) who love to cook can cook extra lunch portions and post them meals on Meet & Lunch. Foodies can order a homemade lunch from Meet&Lunch. Lunch sharing is a natural way to meet other nearby professionals. Homemade food brings people together.

Use Cases

User Case 1

PROBLEM: Sofie wants to get a new job. She has been sending out emails but receives very few responses. Sofie wants to meet with other graphic designers to learn more about the job martket in Boston, but she does not have any friends or connections here.

SOLUTION: Sofie used Meet and Lunch and searched for chefs in her working area. Sofie found Chef Lisa is a senior graphic designer. Sofie ordered a lunch from Lisa and had lunch with Lisa. Sofie had a great time chatting with Lisa and Lisa offered to refer Sofie to Lisa's company.
